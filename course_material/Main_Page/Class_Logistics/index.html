Video Lectures

Are provided via this page (first link of every week under Material), to be watched on Mondays. These are pre-recorded, so should be available well before their respective weeks. The videos typically discuss theory, go through fundamentals of the algorithms, and provide some application examples. Their goal is self-paced learning on one hand (their material is best understood when re-watching is an option). On the other hand, they provide required knowledge for the in-person lectures; this way, youâ€™re guaranteed to get the most out of these.

In-person Lectures

Recordings (old): some minor overlap with the in-person lectures notwithstanding, you may want to watch these. This series of interactive lectures were somewhat unstructured; a mix of answering questions from the discussion board, providing some additional contextual information, discussion points and practical examples (both in research and practice), and discussing example exam questions. Recordings of lectures are handled and prioritized by TU/e. I am currently not aware of them being scheduled for this year. If this does turn out to be the case, I will inform you.
The in-person lectures bridge the content of the video lectures (theory) to its broader application in research. The interim assignment focuses on a specific dataset and task, and these lectures will offer important contextual information to tackle this task; how to include knowledge from the video lectures, but also to offer components of a research workflow that will aid you in writing an NLP research paper.
If you have questions about theory, the most effective place to ask them is after class. Dr. ir. ten Thij may opt to stick around to answer them while the lab session is running.
This year, lectures have been scheduled to take place in Tilburg in the Cobbenhagen building room CZ 120. Please note, the Cobbenhagen building is quite the maze, so please schedule some additional time to find the lecture hall for the first lecture!

Lab sessions

A note, since it seems not to be the norm: provided there is an acceptable turnout for these sessions, I (Marijn) will be also typically be present to answer questions.
The lab sessions will have you complete various formative practical assignments in class. These will be in Python. Extensive knowledge of Python internals is not required for this class, as we will mostly be using libraries. It is, however, recommended you have the basics down. The solutions and a self-grader are available on the assignment pages (Notion). The practicals test your understanding of the coding required for the research paper assignment, so it is recommended you complete them in time. Please bring your own laptop if you intend to work here.
This year, these sessions have been scheduled to take place in Tilburg in the Cobbenhagen building room CZ 120.
These sessions are the most effective place to ask questions about coding, tools, and other matters involving the research paper assignment.
